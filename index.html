The Sunken Shrine of the Sea Serpent

A D&D Mini-Campaign for 3 Players (Levels 1-5)

Dungeon Master's Guide

This mini-campaign is designed for three players and should take characters from level 1 to level 5. The overall story involves investigating strange occurrences in a coastal village, discovering a hidden shrine, and ultimately confronting a powerful sea serpent awakened by foolish treasure hunters.

  • Themes: Exploration, investigation, environmentalism vs. greed, classic good vs. evil.
  • Tone: Starts with a mystery, evolves into an action-adventure with a touch of horror.
  • Advancement: Players should level up at the end of each chapter.

Non-Player Characters (NPCs)

  • Elder Elara (Halfling, female, wise woman/village elder)

    Personality: Kind, concerned, deeply connected to the ocean.

    Goal: Protect the village and the balance of nature.

    Roleplaying Tips: Speak with a calm, soothing voice. Emphasize the importance of respecting the sea.

  • Barnaby "Barny" Crabtree (Gnome, male, enthusiastic but clumsy treasure hunter)

    Personality: Overly excited, driven by greed, not particularly bright.

    Goal: Find treasure and become rich.

    Roleplaying Tips: Speak quickly and loudly. Use grandiose language.

  • Captain Amelia "Stormblade" Thorne (Human, female, seasoned sea captain)

    Personality: Gruff, experienced, loyal to her crew.

    Goal: Ensure the safety of her ship and crew, maintain her reputation.

    Roleplaying Tips: Use nautical terms frequently. Act decisive and authoritative.

Chapter 1: Whispers from the Deep

Objective

Investigate strange happenings in the coastal village of Oakhaven.

Starting Situation

The players arrive in Oakhaven. The village is small, primarily sustained by fishing. Recently, the fishermen have been returning empty-handed, reporting unnatural storms and unsettling whispers on the wind. The players can find lodging at the "Salty Siren" tavern.

Major Events

  • Meeting Elder Elara

    Elder Elara approaches the party, seeking assistance. She explains that the sea has been restless, and the village's livelihood is threatened. She suspects something has disturbed the natural balance. She asks the party to investigate the coastline and nearby islands.

  • Investigating the Coastline

    The party finds strange, unnatural seaweed washed ashore, and dead fish covered in strange, glowing algae. A successful Nature check (DC 10) reveals that the algae is not native to the area and is highly toxic.

  • Encounter with Barnaby Crabtree

    The party encounters Barnaby Crabtree and his small crew (2 bandits). Barnaby is boastful about finding a "secret treasure map" that will lead them to riches. He is secretive about the map’s exact location. The party can choose to:

    • Intimidate Barnaby: (Intimidation check DC 13) to learn more about his map.
    • Persuade Barnaby: (Persuasion check DC 15) to share the map in exchange for assistance.
    • Fight Barnaby: Barnaby and his crew attack if the players attempt to steal the map.

  • The Map

    The map (whether obtained peacefully or through combat) leads to a small, uncharted island a few miles offshore.

Potential Player Choices

  • Focus on helping the village or pursuing personal gain.
  • Trust or distrust Barnaby Crabtree.
  • How to deal with the bandits: capture, kill, or let them go.

Climax

The party charters a boat (Captain Stormblade is available for 50 gold pieces) to the uncharted island.

Chapter 2: The Sunken Shrine

Objective

Explore the uncharted island and discover the source of the disturbance.

Starting Situation

The party arrives at the island. The island is small, rocky, and covered in dense vegetation. A hidden cove leads to a submerged entrance.

Major Events

  • Island Exploration

    The party explores the island, encountering:

    • Giant Crabs: (2) guarding the entrance to the submerged shrine.
    • Hidden Cove: A successful Perception check (DC 12) reveals a hidden cove leading to an underwater entrance.

  • The Sunken Shrine

    The underwater entrance leads to a partially flooded shrine dedicated to a forgotten sea god. The shrine is crumbling and covered in the same glowing algae found on the coastline.

    • Puzzle: A series of pressure plates must be activated in the correct order ( clues are found in the shrine's murals – successful History check DC 14). Activating the plates opens a passage deeper into the shrine.

  • Traps

    The shrine is guarded by traps:

    • Poison Dart Traps: (DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or take 2d6 poison damage).
    • Water-Filled Chamber: (requires swimming and a Strength (Athletics) check DC 12 to navigate).

  • Chamber of Awakening

    The party discovers a chamber where Barnaby Crabtree and his crew are attempting to loot a large, ornate chest. Opening the chest releases a surge of energy, fully awakening the Sea Serpent. Barnaby and his crew are immediately killed by the surge.

Potential Player Choices

  • How to deal with the traps: disarm them, trigger them, or find a way around them.
  • Attempt to stop Barnaby from opening the chest (requires quick action and a successful Persuasion/Intimidation check DC 15).
  • Study the murals: Discover the sea serpent's origin.

Climax

The Sea Serpent awakens, and the chamber begins to flood rapidly. The party must escape the shrine before it collapses or they drown.

Chapter 3: Wrath of the Serpent

Objective

Defeat the Sea Serpent and restore balance to the ocean.

Starting Situation

The party barely escapes the collapsing shrine.

Major Events

  1. Return to Oakhaven

    Exhausted and shaken, the party returns to Oakhaven. They arrive to find the villagers preparing for the worst, having witnessed strange tidal surges and unnatural weather patterns. The party has a chance to warn Elder Elara and the other villagers about the Sea Serpent. This allows the villagers to reinforce their defenses and prepare for evacuation.

  2. The Serpent Strikes

    A day later, the Sea Serpent attacks Oakhaven. The attack begins with a massive wave that crashes into the village, followed by the serpent itself emerging from the depths.

    • Glowing Algae Connection: It becomes clear that the glowing algae is a manifestation of the Sea Serpent's corrupting influence, fueling its power and poisoning the waters.

  3. Defending Oakhaven

    The party helps the villagers defend against the Sea Serpent's attacks. This could involve:

    • Rescuing trapped villagers.
    • Reinforcing barricades.
    • Fighting off smaller creatures summoned by the Sea Serpent (Sea Spawn).

  4. The Ritual to Weaken the Serpent

    Elder Elara reveals a ritual that can weaken the Sea Serpent, making it vulnerable to attack. Performing the ritual requires:

    • Gathering the Herbs: The herbs needed for the ritual are:
      • Seablight Blossom: Grows on the cliffs overlooking the sea. Gathering them requires navigating treacherous terrain and avoiding the Sea Serpent's gaze (Stealth check DC 12).
      • Deeproot Kelp: Found in underwater caves. Retrieving it requires diving into the cold water and fighting off aquatic creatures (Sahuagin).
    • Chanting the Ancient Words: The ancient words are in Aquan, a language understood by few. A character with knowledge of Aquan (such as Aella, if she is present) might know some Aquan from their studies. Alternatively, the party can find a translation in the Oakhaven library with a successful Investigation check (DC 15). Mispronouncing the words has consequences: the ritual could backfire, empowering the serpent instead.
    • Protecting Elara: While Elara performs the ritual, the party must protect her from the Sea Serpent's attacks and summoned creatures.

  5. Confronting the Sea Serpent

    After the ritual, the party confronts the weakened Sea Serpent in a final battle.

    • The Sea Serpent: (Use Water Elemental stat block, but increase its hit points by 25%.). Weakness: Lightning damage. Resistance: Cold damage. The Sea Serpent is now vulnerable after the ritual, taking an additional 1d6 of lightning damage per hit.
    • Environmental Challenges: The battle takes place in the water and on the collapsing docks, creating difficult terrain.

Potential Player Choices

  • Focus on protecting the villagers or directly attacking the Sea Serpent.
  • How to utilize the environment to their advantage.
  • Whether to trust and aid Elder Elara.

Climax

The party defeats the Sea Serpent. With the serpent vanquished, the sea calms, and the glowing algae disappears, signifying the end of its corrupting influence. The villagers celebrate their saviors.

Rewards

  • The gratitude of the villagers of Oakhaven.
  • A magical item recovered from the Sea Serpent's hoard (e.g., a trident that grants control over water, a necklace that provides resistance to cold damage).
  • Level up to level 5.

DM Notes on Player Characters

  • Finn Swiftfoot

    To manage Finn's flaw ("When I see something valuable, I can’t think about anything but how to steal it"), consider introducing opportunities for Finn to use his skills for the party's benefit. For example, he could be tasked with discreetly gathering information or acquiring necessary items without resorting to outright theft. Reward him for creative solutions that align with the party's goals.

  • Torvin Stonebeard

    Torvin’s Favored Enemy has been broadened to "Humanoids (Coastal Raiders and Treasure Hunters)" to ensure that his abilities remain relevant throughout the campaign. This change allows for more flexibility in encounters and provides opportunities for Torvin to shine.

Player Characters

Aella Seawalker

Background & Personality

Appearance: Aella has long, wavy auburn hair and bright green eyes. She wears simple but clean vestments and always carries her holy symbol.

Species: Half-Elf

Class: Cleric (Life Domain) Level 1

Alignment: Lawful Good

Background: Acolyte

Personality Traits:
I am tolerant of other faiths and respect their worship. I quote sacred texts and proverbs in almost every situation.
Ideal:
Charity. I always try to help those in need, no matter the personal cost. (Good)
Bond:
Everything I do is for the common people.
Flaw:
I am inflexible in my thinking.

Features & Abilities

Species Traits (Half-Elf): Darkvision, Fey Ancestry, Skill Versatility.

Background Feature (Acolyte): Shelter of the Faithful - You and your companions can receive free healing and care at a temple, shrine, or other place of worship.

Class Features (Cleric L1):

  • Divine Domain: Life Domain (grants additional healing abilities - Disciple of Life: when you use a spell of 1st level or higher to restore hit points to a creature, the creature regains additional hit points equal to 2 + the spell's level).
  • Spellcasting: Can prepare and cast cleric spells.

Combat Stats (L1)

Armor Class: 18 (Scale Mail, Shield)

Hit Points: 9 (Hit Dice: 1d8)

Speed: 30 ft.

STR13 (+1)
DEX10 (+0)
CON14 (+2)
INT8 (-1)
WIS15 (+2)
CHA12 (+1)

Proficiency Bonus: +2

Saving Throws: Wisdom +4, Charisma +3

Skills: Insight +4, Religion +1. (Half-elf skill versatility used elsewhere or implied by choices)

Equipment

Primary Weapon: Mace

Other Key Items: Holy Symbol, Shield, Scale Mail.

Full List: A holy symbol, Prayer book, 5 sticks of incense, Vestments, 15 gp, Mace, Shield, Scale Mail, Explorer's Pack.

Languages: Common, Elvish, Celestial.

Story Integration

Aella's devotion to her faith and healing abilities make her a valuable asset in helping the villagers of Oakhaven and combating the Sea Serpent. Her knowledge of Aquan could also be vital during the ritual.

Finn Swiftfoot

Background & Personality

Appearance: Finn is a small, wiry halfling with a mischievous grin and nimble fingers. He wears practical, dark clothing.

Species: Halfling

Class: Rogue Level 1

Alignment: Chaotic Neutral

Background: Criminal

Personality Traits:
I always have a plan for what to do when things go wrong. I am always calm, no matter what the situation. I never lose my cool.
Ideal:
Freedom. Chains are meant to be broken, as are those who would forge them. (Chaotic)
Bond:
I’m trying to pay off an old debt I owe to a generous benefactor.
Flaw:
When I see something valuable, I can’t think about anything but how to steal it.

Features & Abilities

Species Traits (Halfling): Lucky, Brave, Halfling Nimbleness.

Background Feature (Criminal): Criminal Contact - You have a reliable and trustworthy contact who acts as your liaison to a network of other criminals.

Class Features (Rogue L1):

  • Expertise: Choose two skill proficiencies (e.g., Stealth, Thieves' Tools) to double the proficiency bonus.
  • Sneak Attack (1d6): Deal extra 1d6 damage when attacking with advantage or if an ally is within 5ft of the target.
  • Thieves' Cant: A secret language used by rogues.

Combat Stats (L1)

Armor Class: 14 (Leather Armor, Dex)

Hit Points: 10 (Hit Dice: 1d8)

Speed: 25 ft.

STR10 (+0)
DEX15 (+2)
CON14 (+2)
INT12 (+1)
WIS8 (-1)
CHA13 (+1)

Proficiency Bonus: +2

Saving Throws: Dexterity +4, Intelligence +3

Skills: Acrobatics +4, Deception +3, Insight -1, Stealth +4. (Proficiencies: Deception, Stealth from Criminal; Acrobatics, Insight from Rogue)

Tool Proficiencies: Thieves' Tools.

Equipment

Primary Weapon: Shortbow

Other Key Items: Thieves' Tools, Bone Dice, Two daggers.

Full List: A set of bone dice, A set of common clothes, 15 gp, Leather Armor, Two daggers, Thieves’ tools, Shortbow and 20 arrows.

Languages: Common, Halfling, Thieves' Cant.

Story Integration

Finn's skills in stealth and deception can be useful for gathering information and navigating the traps within the Sunken Shrine. The DM can use his flaw to create opportunities for him to discreetly gather information or acquire necessary items, rewarding creative solutions.

Torvin Stonebeard

Background & Personality

Appearance: Torvin is a sturdy dwarf with a long, braided beard and weathered skin. He wears practical leather armor and carries a longbow.

Species: Dwarf

Class: Ranger Level 1

Alignment: Neutral Good

Background: Outlander

Personality Traits:
I am always eager to see the other side of the hill. I place no stock in wealthy or well-mannered folk. Money and manners won’t save you from a hungry owlbear.
Ideal:
Greater Good. My gifts are meant to be shared with all. (Good)
Bond:
I protect those who cannot protect themselves.
Flaw:
I am too enamored of ale, wine, and other intoxicants.

Features & Abilities

Species Traits (Dwarf): Darkvision, Dwarven Resilience, Dwarven Combat Training, Stonecunning.

Background Feature (Outlander): Wanderer - You have an excellent memory for maps and geography, and you can always recall the general layout of terrain, settlements, and other features around you. (Also proficiency in one musical instrument).

Class Features (Ranger L1):

  • Favored Enemy: Humanoids (Coastal Raiders and Treasure Hunters). You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track them, as well as on Intelligence checks to recall information about them.
  • Natural Explorer: Coast. You gain several benefits when traveling for an hour or more in your favored terrain (difficult terrain doesn't slow group, can't get lost except by magic, remain alert to danger even when doing other things, etc.).

CON15 (+2)
INT8 (-1)
WIS12 (+1)
CHA10 (+0)

Proficiency Bonus: +2

Saving Throws: Strength +4, Dexterity +3

Skills: Athletics +4, Survival +3. (From Outlander. Ranger choices: Animal Handling, Nature, Perception)

Equipment

Primary Weapon: Longbow

Other Key Items: Hunting Trap, Token from Fallen Enemy, Shortsword, Scale mail armor.

Story Integration

Torvin's knowledge of the wilderness and tracking skills make him ideal for investigating the coastline and hunting the Sea Serpent. His favored enemy (Coastal Raiders and Treasure Hunters) will make him particularly driven to deal with Barnaby and his crew.
